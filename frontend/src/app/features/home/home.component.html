About the Platform

What is a DAO?

A Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) is an organization governed by smart contracts and community voting. Members hold governance tokens that grant them voting rights on proposals that shape the organization's future.

Governance Token (DGOV)

DGOV is the governance token that powers this DAO. Holding DGOV tokens allows you to:

  • Vote on proposals
  • Create new proposals
  • Delegate voting power to others
  • Participate in treasury decisions

Voting Power

Your voting power is determined by the amount of DGOV tokens you hold. You can increase your voting power by locking tokens for a specified period. Longer lock periods result in greater voting power.

Proposals

Proposals are suggestions for changes or actions the DAO should take. They can include:

  • Treasury fund allocation
  • Protocol parameter changes
  • Strategic decisions
  • Community initiatives

Once a proposal is created, it goes through a voting period where token holders can vote For, Against, or Abstain. If a proposal reaches quorum and passes, it can be executed on-chain.

Treasury

The DAO treasury holds the organization's funds. All treasury transactions require multi-signature approval to ensure security. The community votes on how treasury funds should be spent.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need tokens to participate?

Yes, you need DGOV tokens to vote on proposals and participate in governance. You can view proposals and DAO statistics without tokens.

How do I get voting power?

Hold DGOV tokens in your wallet. You can increase your voting power by locking tokens in the vote escrow contract.

Can I change my vote?

No, once you cast a vote on a proposal, it cannot be changed. Make sure to review the proposal carefully before voting.

What is vote delegation?

Vote delegation allows you to assign your voting power to another address. This is useful if you trust someone else to vote on your behalf.

How long does the voting period last?

The voting period is set by the governance parameters. You can view the current settings in the Governance section.
